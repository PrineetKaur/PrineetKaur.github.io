About Me

I bring nearly a decade of experience working across knowledge systems, content design, and technical documentation (making complex products easier to understand and use through clear information architecture, structured knowledge, and scalable content experiences).

MY PHILOSOPHY:

"Good content is not just well-written; it's well-architected. It should work well at every layer: clear enough for users to act on, structured enough for teams to scale, and precise enough for AI to reason over.”

My work has always combined the craft of content with the rigour of systems thinking (shaping in-product content, documentation, and onboarding journeys that improve clarity, reduce friction, and drive self-serve adoption).

And that meant working not just at the level of what users read or understand, but at the level of the structures, frameworks, and infrastructure that make consistent, high-quality content possible at scale (including how AI can extend that consistency further).

In my free time, I enjoy hands-on technical experiments and open-source contributions (on GitHub). I have a strong interest in technical storytelling, and regularly write (on Medium) with a focus on making emerging technologies more accessible and practical.

Outside of work, I enjoy staying active. (Since school days, I’ve been involved in athletics, participated in marathons, and represented my state twice at national-level tournament).
