About Me

I come with a decades' experience of innovating and building at the crossroads of product, engineering, and data (delivering ambitious roadmaps, and untangling unstructured territoires within fast-paced scale-ups).

What Drives Me?

“Turning technical chaos into clear, scalable products”, and “Helping teams ship things they’re genuinely proud of.”

My career so far has been a blend of managing innovative technology products and leading complex technical programs. And lately, my work has revolved around building developer-focused products (Developer Experience, Infrastructure, Platform Capabilities) and data platforms (Data Science, Engineering, Analytics).

Along the way, I’ve collaborated closely with Platform/Data Engineering, System Architects, and DevOps teams. And these are the spaces I’m the happiest, contributing to "How Engineers build, and use Technical Products". Further, I love to tinker with hands-on experiments (on Github) keeping me alive in the open source community.

Somewhere between all of that, I also developed a soft spot for technical storytelling. Sharing my own experiences and learnings (on Medium) through blogs on the latest technological advancements and technical writeups (e.g. how-to guides for developer onboarding or breaking down complex concepts in a way that lands).

Outside of work, I love to keep myself active and fit. (Since my school days, I have been an athlete, won many medals, participated in marathons, and even represented my state twice at national-level tournament).

My Philosophy is Simple:

Build smart, Deliver well, Stay curious, and Make Technology Understandable.
